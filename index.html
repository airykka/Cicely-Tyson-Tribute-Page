Cicely Tyson

A trailblazer whose legacy couldn’t be measured by her Emmys and Tony and Oscar alone, but by the barriers she broke and the dreams she made possible”.
— Barrack Obama

Cicely Tyson. "You made me feel loved and seen and valued in a world where there is still a cloak of invisibility for us dark chocolate girls." -Viola Davis. Photograph: Harpo/PA

Cicely Tyson was an award-winning film, television and stage actress known for playing strong Black leads and refusing to play roles that were demeaning to Black women. The actor has long been considered a force in the entertainment industry, opening doors for many Black professionals to follow.

Tyson had a well-known commitment to community involvement. She co-founded the Dance Theater of Harlem after Martin Luther King Jr.'s assassination, and when a school board in East Orange, New Jersey, wanted to name a performing arts school after her, she only agreed to accept the honor if she could participate in school activities. In addition to attending meetings and events, Tyson even taught a master class at the school.

Unless a piece really said something, I had no interest in it. I have got to know that I have served some purpose here.

– Cicely Tyson

Awards & Honors

1963 - 1974

  • First African American star of a TV drama
  • Nominated for an Academy Award
  • Won two Emmy Awards

1977 - 1982

  • Became a member of the Black Filmmakers Hall of Fame
  • Awarded the Golden Plate Award of the American Academy of Achievement
  • Women in Film Crystal Award

1983 - 1994

  • Lead actress in The Corn Is Green movie
  • Candace Award for Distinguished Service from the National Coalition of 100 Black Women
  • Recieved her third Emmy award

2010 - 2013

  • Awarded the 95th Spingarn Medal by the NAACP
  • Won awards for her apperance in The Help (2011) as maid Constantine Bates
  • Recieved her Tony Award for best performance by an actress in a leading role in a play

2015 - 2017

  • Nominated for an Emmy for her guest-starring role in ABC's How to Get Away With Murder
  • Recipient of the Kennedy Center Honors
  • Honored with the Presidential Medal of Freedom by Barack Obama

2018 - 2020

  • First African-American woman to receive an honorary Oscar
  • Inducted into the American Theater Hall of Fame in the same year
  • Inducted into the Television Academy's Hall of Fame
  • Career Achievement Peabody award

“I have managed Miss Tyson’s career for over 40 years, and each year was a privilege and blessing. The day she died, she placed the last ornament, a Star, on top of the tree.”
- Larry Thompson
